Introduction

Background

We have conducted 8 qualitative studies on women leaders in South Korea over the past years using semi-structured interviews. Based on the 200 interviews data collected from the 10 qualitative studies, we will reanalyze the interview data using the topic modeling method to see if there are any research topics and themes that are missed by using the qualitative research method only.

Purpose of Research

The purpose of this study is to reanalyze the interview data that have been collected in 8 qualitative studies over the past 8 years, using the topic modeling method that is used to tackle the subjectivity issue in qualitative research using computational techniques to identify key research topics on women leaders in South Korea and to provide implications for research and practice for the future.

Method

Sample

As this study is a secondary analysis of the 200 interviews data collected using the topic modeling method, we will not contact any research subjects, and thus, there is not risk involved.

Analytic Procedure

  1. We will anonymize all the 200 interviews data (transcripts) collected and create metadata information including: interview participant numbers (2015-1 to 2015-18, 2016-1 to 2016-50, 2017-1 to 2017-19, 2018-1 to 2018-21, 2019-1 to 2019-15, 2020-1 to 2020-3, 2021-1 to 2021-23, 2022-1 to 2022-50), participants’ industries/fields, positions, years of work, the number of children, and family support. This anonymized metadata information will be used to compare emerged patterns of woman in leadership by demographic attributes only.
  2. The topic modeling method will be applied to a few sample interviews data before determining the fit of the topic modeling method to all interviews data. If there is missing information in the metadata information, we will discuss about how to fill the gap and proceed.
  3. When we reach consensus, the topic modeling method will be applied to all interviews data using computational techniques (see the data analysis plan below).
  4. As the results of the computational process are generated, all researchers will discuss about the validity and credibility of the results from topic modeling, and to do so, findings from previous eight studies will be compared. Five researchers who had participated in all or some qualitative studies and two researchers who didn’t will go through the validation process, and in the process, we will identify key research topics on women leaders in South Korea. Through using the topic modeling method which is a computational approach to unstructured qualitative data collected, we aim to identify hidden research topics that we could not find using qualitative research methods only. Combining computational and interpretive approaches in the process of using topic modeling, we will provide much more unbiased, reliable, and reproducible research topics that will open the possibility for uncharted future investigation and implications for practice.

The data analysis process is comprised of four primary steps: data collection and restructuring, data cleanup, preprocessing, and modeling. First, in the data collection and restructuring phase, the 200 interview transcripts will be converted to text files. For the reframing procedure, transformed interview transcripts will be saved in an Excel file using the data frame format. We will create a separate cell for interview questions. In the data frame, an interviewee’s answer will be separated into paragraph units, and then the information of the interviewee that has been anonymized will be matched with interview questions. This process is essential for determining the unit of analysis in topic modeling. The second step is data cleanup. R (statistic software for text mining) will be loaded with a data frame containing the metadata of interviewees, interview questions, and responses. In the process of data cleanup, data validation verifies the datapoint’s type and identifies any missing data. Third, In comparison to typical qualitative and quantitative data, text mining’s pre-processing is the most distinctive procedure. In this pre-processing phase, the text of our target data will be turned into a corpus of electronic text documents for analysis. In this step, n-gram (generating a list of one word, two words, etc.), stop words (words that provide little information for modeling (i.e., a, an, the, and. . . ), and part-of-speech tags are examined to construct a word list (tokenizing). Lastly, in the modeling stage, a structured topic modeling (STM) based on the LDA (Latent Dirichlet Allocation) method will be performed using information, such as corpus, word list (token), and metadata generated in the pre-processing stage. Topic modeling returns a series of word lists for each topic (the number of topics will be specified) and each topic’s occurrence probability. It also provides multiple selection criteria for optimal numbers of topics for us to evaluate. Using the final model, researchers will label topics and describe them based on co-appearance of keywords within and across topics to summarize the entire text

Contribution

This study will greatly contribute to identifying more unbiased, reliable, and reproducible research topics through reanalysis of the 200 interviews data using the topic modeling method and provide implications for research and practice for the future.
